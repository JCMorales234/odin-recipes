Description

Patacones are fried green plantain patties that are a popular side dish or snack across Colombia. They are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. When served with hogao (a traditional Colombian tomato and onion sauce) and grated cheese, they become a delicious and satisfying appetizer or light meal. This is a simple but incredibly flavorful dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 green plantains.
  • Frying oil (like vegetable or canola oil).
  • Salt to taste.
  • For the Hogao: 1 large tomato, 1/2 onion, salt, pepper, and a pinch of cumin.
  • To serve: grated white cheese (like queso costeÃ±o or mozzarella).
  • Optional: cilantro for garnish.

Steps

  1. Peel the green plantains and cut them into thick slices (about 1-2 inches).
  2. Heat a generous amount of oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Fry the plantain slices until they are soft and a light yellow color (about 2-3 minutes per side). Do not let them get too brown or crispy yet.
  3. Remove the plantains from the oil and, using a plate, a wooden board, or a special tool called a tostonera, smash each piece into a flat, round patty.
  4. Put the flattened plantain patties back into the hot oil. This time, fry them until they are golden brown and very crispy (about 2 minutes per side). Remove and drain on a paper towel. Season with salt immediately.
  5. While the patacones are frying, finely chop the tomato and onion. SautÃ© them in a small pan with a little oil until they form a soft sauce. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of cumin.
  6. Place the hot, crispy patacones on a plate. Top them with a spoonful of the warm hogao and a generous sprinkle of grated cheese. Serve immediately.
