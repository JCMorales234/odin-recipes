Description

bandeja paisa is a traditional Colombian platter from the Antioquia region, known for its generous and hearty portions. The dish typically includes a variety of foods, such as grilled steak, fried pork rind (chicharrÃ³n), chorizo, a fried egg, rice, red beans, and sweet plantains. It's often served with a side of avocado and an arepa, making it a complete and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked red beans
  • 1 cup white rice
  • 1 grilled steak (about 6 oz)
  • 1 fried egg
  • 1 chorizo sausage
  • 1 piece of fried pork belly (chicharrÃ³n)
  • 1 plantain
  • 1 small avocado, sliced
  • 1 arepa (corn cake)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the Red Beans (Frijoles)
  2. Cook the pork belly in a pan until it's super crispy and golden. The key is to cook it low and slow so the fat melts and the skin gets bubbly and crunchy.
  3. Quickly cook a thin steak on a grill or in a pan.
  4. Cook the sausages until they are browned and done all the way through.
  5. Make a pot of plain white rice.
  6. Slice up a ripe plantain and fry it until it's golden and sweet.
  7. Fry an egg so the yolk is still runny.
  8. Warm up a small corn cake (arepa).
  9. Put it all together in a big plate
  10. Don't forget to add a slice of avocado on the side!
