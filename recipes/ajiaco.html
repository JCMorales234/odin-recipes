Description

Ajiaco is a thick, creamy soup from BogotÃ¡ and the surrounding Cundinamarca region. Its unique texture comes from a mix of three types of potatoes (creole, pastusa, and sabanera), which all cook down differently. The soup is made with chicken, corn on the cob, and a special herb called guascas, which gives it its distinct flavor. It's traditionally served with a side of cream, capers, and a slice of avocado. This is a complete and comforting meal, perfect for a cold day.

Ingredients (for 4 people)

  • 4 boneless chicken breasts.
  • 3 ears of corn, cut into pieces.
  • 3 types of potatoes (about 2.2 lbs total): criolla (this type will dissolve and thicken the soup), pastusa and sabanera (these will stay firm).
  • 1 bunch of guascas (a key herb, often found in Latin American grocery stores).
  • 1 green onion and 1 bunch of cilantro.
  • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • To serve: heavy cream, capers, and avocado.

Steps

  1. In a large pot, place the chicken breasts, corn, green onion, and cilantro. Cover with water, season with salt and pepper, and cook until the chicken is tender (about 30-40 minutes).
  2. While the chicken cooks, peel and slice all the potatoes. The criolla potatoes are the most important for thickening the soup.
  3. Remove the chicken and corn from the pot. Shred the chicken and set it aside. Add all the sliced potatoes and the guascas to the remaining broth.
  4. Let the soup simmer on medium-low heat. The criolla potatoes will break down, giving the ajiaco its creamy texture. This should take about 20-30 minutes.
  5. Add the shredded chicken and corn back into the pot. Serve the soup in deep bowls, and garnish each one with a dollop of heavy cream, a spoonful of capers, and a slice of avocado.
